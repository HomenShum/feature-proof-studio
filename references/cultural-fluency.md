# Cultural fluency — recognition before persuasion

A walkthrough proves the product works. For high-trust audiences (founders, family offices,
wealth advisors, M&A teams, private-client operators), that is necessary but not sufficient:
they buy from people who **understand the room they sit in**. The content must not only say
"here is the service" — it must say *"I understand the world this service belongs inside."*
Virality gets attention; recognition builds trust; trust creates access.

## The three-layer content stack (every walkthrough/episode, in order)
1. **Product proof** — what did you build? (the captured walkthrough, the code, the evidence)
2. **Context recognition** — what real situation does this belong to? (a scene from THEIR world:
   the IC meeting prep, the investor update, the pre-meeting client briefing)
3. **Cultural fluency** — what does this audience care about beneath the task? (time, discretion,
   provenance, continuity, reputation — not features)

Weak: "Multiple users and agents collaborate on a spreadsheet."
Fluent: "A private investment team is reviewing a founder before Monday's IC meeting. The risk
isn't whether AI can write cells — it's whether the team can trust who changed what, why, and
from which source."

## The fluency grid (pick the audience BEFORE writing the spec)
| Audience | Publicly talk about | Privately care about | Walkthrough scene | Trust signal to SHOW |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Family offices | private markets, governance, succession, philanthropy, AI | discretion, continuity, advisor coordination | IC research room | provenance, permissions, audit trail |
| Founders / execs | growth, fundraising, AI leverage | time, reputation, board clarity | investor-update prep | evidence-backed memo |
| Wealth advisors | client service, portfolio review | trust, retention, responsiveness | pre-meeting briefing | explainability, review workflow |
| Boutique M&A | diligence, CIMs, comparables | speed, accuracy, version control | live diligence sheet | source-linked outputs |
| High-touch operators | personalization, clienteling | taste, memory, continuity | client context room | subtle personalization, no creepiness |
| Next-gen wealth | AI, private markets, impact | autonomy, education, legitimacy | family wiki + decision journal | clarity without condescension |

## Lexicon discipline
**Use naturally:** principal · advisor · investment committee · diligence memo · liquidity event ·
private markets · approval workflow · audit trail · provenance · discretion · continuity.
**Never:** elite · premium · exclusive · VIP · "luxury AI" · watches/yachts/jets imagery.
The aesthetic is **quiet competence, not loud wealth** — luxury texture lives in pacing,
typography, calm, restraint; the substance stays operational.

## Audience context file
Capture the audience's world as data (`audience_context.example.yaml` in this folder): cultural
values, repeated questions, recognizable artifacts, product mapping, lexicon, and
`trust_signals_required` that the DEMO must show (not narrate): fixture data only, provenance
visible, review-before-action on screen.

## Cultural-fluency eval (run before publishing; any 0 blocks)
Score 0–2 with evidence: **context accuracy** (would this scenario happen in their world?) ·
**language fluency** (native, not forced) · **status restraint** (no cheap luxury signaling) ·
**trust awareness** (privacy/discretion/review addressed) · **decision relevance** (real
decisions, not generic productivity) · **proof quality** (credible evidence shown).

## The trust ladder content moves people down
Recognition → Competence → Taste → Proof → Discretion → Conversation ("I'd take a meeting").

## Grounding
